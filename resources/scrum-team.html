Weekly Report

In software development, weekly update meetings (sometimes called "standup" or "scrum" meetings) are a common occurrence. These meetings are brief, complete accountability reports of each team member's weekly progress. Awareness to problems can be raised...but this is not the place to solve them.

A standup will generally involve each person taking about a minute to answer 3 questions:

  1. What did I do since the last meeting?
  2. What am I going to do next?
  3. What are my blockers?

Blockers are anything that are keeping you from being successful in your goals. They are often things outside of your control, but can also include troublesome bugs, broken code, design questions that you just can't come up with a solution for, they can even be other people. "I just didn't get around to it..." is not a blocker...it is however a good way to get removed from projects and ultimately fired ;)
