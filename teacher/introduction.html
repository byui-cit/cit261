Instructor Preparation

Course outline

The course is broken up into two units. Unit one is weeks 1-6 and consists of 4 weeks of reading and completing exercises from the book, then a two week application where the students will complete a self selected small coding project. They should plan on about 15 hours for their project spent over weeks 5 and 6.

Team Activities

During most weeks the students will have a required team activity that they should complete in a peer programming style. You can decide whether to form the groups yourself, or allow the students to self select. They should plan on a 1 hour block per week where they will need to meet to complete the activity. I recommend 3-4 students per group. Most of the points for these activities come from participation...only about 10% or so is for completion.

Unit 2

Unit two is weeks 7-14 and is made up of another 3 weeks of reading and exercises and then 4 weeks of application. The students again will select an application that they would like to build. They should spend more time preparing a project plan this time however. If they did not get everything done on the first coding challenge that they wanted to and there is enough left to do I think it would be fine to allow them to continue the unit one project into unit 2. That is up to you. They should plan on 30 hours for this project.

On the reading weeks remind the students often that if they only read through the material once and then move on they will not gain the understanding they need and will struggle in other parts of the course. They should not look at the assigned readings and say "Great! Two chapters to read...I can finish this week's work in a hour or two and move on!" They should instead plan on reading and re-reading, completing exercises, looking for alternative sources on the material, etc until they have mastered the topic(s).

Instructor Time expectations

The course is designed to minimize the amount of time you spend grading to allow for more time teaching and giving individualized attention to students that need it. Each week the students are required to turn in either notes or worked exercises. You should review those with the goal of extracting questions to address with the class. They will also submit a weekly report of status. Again, review these looking for students whom you may need to reach out to. I would grade both of these on completion. Feel free to dock a few points on the notes if you feel they are putting in minimal effort.

You should plan on holding an office hour each week. Record this for those that can't make it. Use this time to answer any questions you find in the notes or that the students attending bring, and then work on a coding example illustrating skills from the readings. Each week in the notes I'll give an example project...use mine or feel free to come up with your own.

You may also consider preparing a short video introduction for each week to help the students plan their time.

Final grades

The grading for the course is a bit different. 30% of the grade will be made up of the weekly notes and team actities. The other 70% will be based off of a self evaluation that the student performs. They will assess themselves on how well they have achieved the outcomes of the course, prepare a short video with their justifications, and then provide a portfolio of work to back up their claims. You should review their marks and their submitted work and adjust as necessary.
