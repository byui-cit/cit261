Instructor Preparation

Overview

This week will be about review. It will also be about introducing the course. There is no team activity for this week, and most of the student's time will be spent working through the Doing Stuff book. 90% of the material in that book is intended to be review...but it feels new to too many students. They might panic a bit when they pull it down and look at the pages, but once they get into it, they should move quickly.

Make sure the students understand the grading structure. 30% comes from reading and team activities. 70% from self evaluation and portfolio. The self evaluations will happen twice: once at midterm, and once at the end of the semester.

Help them to understand that the readings are important, and they get to choose how to best spend their time. If they learn best by just reading then you should expect detailed notes from them. If they learn best by experimentation and doing, then you should expect exercises that have been worked through. They have the option but should plan on spending adequate time during the week to learn the material.

Encourage them also to look to other sources when things don't make sense. The book does a pretty good job, but the way it addresses some topics might be confusing to some students. Help them to remember that there are other options. Several are listed in this weeks reading in fact. The notes they turn in do not have to be all from the assigned reading.

Encourage the students also to record their questions while reading and give them to you to be addressed.

Meeting Time

For your meeting with them you might consider running through an exercise building a basic html file...talk about the parts and why each are important. Then build and style a basic form as review. The course does not spend a lot of time teaching CSS, but students are expected to style their applications. A review would probably be welcomed by many students.
